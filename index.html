x402 SMS

A paid SMS service for AI agents, billed per-message in USDC.

MCP-compatible x402 protocol USDC on Base

What this service does

x402 SMS is a Model Context Protocol (MCP) server operated by Joseph Henry, sole proprietor. It exposes a single tool — send_sms — that AI agents (Claude, ChatGPT, and other LLM-based systems) can call to send SMS text messages to phone numbers their end users have consented to receive messages from.

Each send_sms call is billed $0.03 USDC, settled on-chain via the x402 payment protocol. No accounts, no pre-funding — payment happens per request.

Who uses this

Software developers and AI application builders who want their AI agents to send transactional SMS notifications without integrating a full CPaaS account. Common use cases include:

  • Order and shipment status notifications
  • Workflow completion alerts
  • System health and monitoring alerts
  • Calendar and meeting reminders
  • Personal task reminders triggered by AI assistants

The service is not intended for marketing, promotional content, political messaging, or any high-risk SHAFT-C (sex, hate, alcohol, firearms, tobacco, cannabis) content.

How opt-in works

Recipient consent is mandatory and is the responsibility of the AI agent operator using this service. Before any SMS is dispatched, the operator must have obtained express written consent from the recipient via one of the following methods:

  • The recipient registered their phone number with the operator's product (website, application, or service) and explicitly opted in to receive SMS notifications.
  • The recipient is the operator themselves (self-notifications to their own number).
  • The recipient provided their number with clear notice of SMS use during a transaction with the operator.

When an agent calls the send_sms tool, the operator must include an opt_in_attestation parameter confirming that the recipient has consented. Our Terms of Service make the operator solely responsible for maintaining records of consent in compliance with the TCPA and applicable state law. Calls without this attestation are rejected.

STOP and HELP keywords

The service automatically honors industry-standard SMS keywords. Recipients can text any of the following words to +1 (888) 841-3236 to control their messages:

  • STOP, STOPALL, UNSUBSCRIBE, CANCEL, END, QUIT — opts the recipient out of all future messages from this number. The opt-out is permanent and applies to all operators using this service.
  • HELP, INFO — returns an automated reply identifying the service and providing the contact email below.
  • START, UNSTOP — re-enables messages for a previously opted-out recipient (requires the original consent to still be valid).

The opt-out list is maintained at the phone-number level and persists across all operators. Once a recipient sends STOP, no further messages will be sent to that number from this service regardless of which operator initiates the call.

Message frequency

Frequency varies by operator and use case. The service is transactional and event-driven — messages are sent in response to discrete events triggered by AI agents on behalf of consenting recipients. Typical recipients receive between 1 and 20 messages per month. The service is not used for recurring scheduled campaigns or bulk broadcasts.

Pricing

$0.03 USDC per SMS message, charged to the AI agent's wallet at the time of the API call via the x402 protocol. No subscription, no minimum spend, no recipient-side fees.

Privacy Policy

x402 SMS collects and processes the following data:

  • Recipient phone numbers — used solely to deliver the requested SMS. Stored for 90 days for delivery confirmation and compliance audit, then deleted.
  • Message content — stored for 30 days for delivery troubleshooting and carrier complaint response, then deleted.
  • Wallet addresses — public blockchain addresses used to settle x402 payments. Retained indefinitely as part of immutable payment records.
  • Opt-out records — phone numbers that have texted STOP are retained indefinitely to honor the opt-out across future requests.

Data is never sold, rented, or shared with third parties except as required by law or to fulfill the SMS delivery itself (Twilio is the carrier-facing service provider).
